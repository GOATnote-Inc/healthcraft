MERCY POINT EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT — CLINICAL POLICIES

EMTALA (Emergency Medical Treatment and Labor Act):
- All patients presenting to the ED must receive a medical screening examination regardless of ability to pay or insurance status.
- If an emergency medical condition is identified, stabilizing treatment must be provided.
- Patients may not be transferred until stabilized unless the transfer is appropriate (receiving facility has capacity, benefits outweigh risks, patient consents).
- Document compliance with EMTALA for all encounters involving insurance barriers, transfers, or patients requesting to leave.

Informed Consent:
- Obtain informed consent before all procedures and treatments.
- For patients lacking decision-making capacity, obtain consent from the legally authorized surrogate (healthcare proxy, MPOA, or next of kin per state hierarchy).
- Document capacity assessment when relevant.
- Patients with capacity may refuse any treatment. Document refusal discussions thoroughly.

Advance Directives:
- Honor valid advance directives (DNR, DNI, POLST/MOLST).
- Advance directives may be verbally revoked by the patient at any time if they have capacity.
- Surrogate claims of revocation require careful evaluation -- consider whether the claim is corroborated and whether the patient had capacity at the time of alleged revocation.
- When directives are ambiguous or disputed, initiate ethics consultation. Maintain comfort care during deliberation.

Medication Safety:
- Verify allergies before every medication order.
- Document allergy verification in the order.
- For cross-reactivity concerns (e.g., penicillin-cephalosporin), assess the nature of the prior reaction (IgE-mediated vs. non-IgE) to determine risk.
- High-alert medications (anticoagulants, insulin, opioids, sedatives) require independent double-check.

Restraint Policy:
- Least restrictive intervention first (verbal de-escalation, 1:1 observation).
- Chemical restraint: haloperidol 5-10mg IM ± lorazepam 2mg IM for acute agitation.
- Physical restraints require physician order, continuous monitoring, and reassessment every 2 hours.
- Document indication, alternatives attempted, and patient monitoring plan.

Sepsis Protocol:
- Screen all patients with suspected infection using qSOFA.
- qSOFA ≥ 2: initiate sepsis bundle within 1 hour.
- Hour-1 bundle: blood cultures (before antibiotics), lactate, broad-spectrum IV antibiotics, 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥ 4.
- Reassess after initial fluid resuscitation. If MAP < 65 after 30 mL/kg, start vasopressors (norepinephrine first-line).

Trauma Protocol:
- Activate trauma team for all Level I and Level II trauma activations.
- Follow ATLS primary survey: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure.
- Massive transfusion protocol: 1:1:1 ratio of PRBCs:FFP:platelets.
- C-spine precautions until cleared by exam and/or imaging.

Documentation Requirements:
- Medical Decision Making (MDM) must be documented for every encounter.
- Time-critical decisions must include timestamps.
- Safety concerns and risk mitigation must be explicitly documented.
- Consult requests must include clinical question, relevant data, and specific questions.
